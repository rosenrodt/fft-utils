  • Algorithm
    1. Perform FFTs along the high dimension D2\bold{D_2}D2​
    2. Pointwise multiply by twiddle factor WN1N2n1k2W^{n_1 k_2}_{N_1 N_2}WN1​N2​n1​k2​​
    3. Perform FFTs along the low dimension D1\bold{D_1}D1​

3-pass decomposition

  • With N=N1N2N3N=N_1N_2N_3N=N1​N2​N3​
    • Fold nnn into (n1,n2,n3)(n_1,n_2,n_3)(n1​,n2​,n3​) where n=n1+N1n2+N1N2n3n=n_1+N_1n_2+N_1N_2n_3n=n1​+N1​n2​+N1​N2​n3​
    • Fold kkk into (k3,k2,k1)(k_3,k_2,k_1)(k3​,k2​,k1​) where k=k3+N3k2+N2N3k1k=k_3+N_3k_2+N_2 N_3k_1k=k3​+N3​k2​+N2​N3​k1​ (dimension reversed/transposed)

  • Recursive tree structure revealed:
    • FFT for N1N2N3N_1 N_2 N_3N1​N2​N3​ array requires 2-pass FFT for N1N_1N1​-by-N2N3N_2 N_3N2​N3​ pseudo matrix and twiddle WN1(N3N2)W_{N_1 (N_3 N_2)}WN1​(N3​N2​)​

Tree decomposition

  • Example input: 256-point signal
  • Decompose into 4-pass radix-4 FFTs
    • Perform radix-4 FFTs along dim D4\bold{D_4}D4​; pointwise-multiply twiddle factor WN3N4W_{N_3 N_4}WN3​N4​​
    • Perform radix-4 FFTs along dim D3\bold{D_3}D3​; pointwise-multiply twiddle factor WN2(N4N3)(!)W_{N_2 (N_4 N_3)^{(!)}}WN2​(N4​N3​)(!)​

Progressive transposition (Stockham algorithm)

  • Instead of transposing all dimensions at once in the end, transpose one at a time as we do the butterfly
  • Improve performance since reads from LDS are always coalesced (see: Geometric View of Stockham Algorithm)
